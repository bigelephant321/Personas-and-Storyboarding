Personas and Storyboarding: An Exploration of CVS Self Checkout

Below, we have a diagram of the CVS self-checkout interface

Here are some some observations I had when watching people use this interface:

  • Users are fairly quick to walk up to an open machine, given the “OPEN” screen when it is not being used
  • Most users intuitively start scanning quickly after starting
  • There are shelves to put items on the right and left of the checkout. One is weighted, and is where items are supposed to go
  • One user put their item on the wrong shelf, and had to get an employee to bypass a screen when the scale did not register weight
  • The user process slows down when users reach the paying stage
  • Users pause to read each screen (donation, payment options) when paying
  • Users are easily able to make the switch from self-checkout screen to card reader, especially when prompted
  • Payment on card reader is smooth

Below are questions I asked them about their experience, and some answers I got

  • Did you struggle with any aspect of the self-checkout experience?
    • “There are a lot of steps to finish and pay, but I didn’t really struggle”
    • “I put my bagged items on the wrong side”
    • “No”
  • Did you have to get employee help for bypassing an issue?
    • “No”
    • “Yes, the machine rang the employee for help when I did not put my items in the right spot”
    • “No”
  • Was the interface easier or harder to navigate then what you would expect for a self-checkout experience?
    • “Pretty standard”
    • “Aside from the employee help part, it was pretty easy”
    • “There are more steps than I would like, but not necessarily difficult”
  • Is there anything missing from the self-checkout experience?
    • “I can’t really think of anything”
    • “Maybe a way to resolve issues by myself? If I put something in the wrong place, I should be able to move it and resolve the problem. Especially if the employees are dealing with other customers this is annoying”
    • “No”
  • SUMMARY: People are able to use the self-checkout pretty easily. There may be some improvements to make in terms of streamlining the checkout process and where to put groceries, but overall satisfactory. The biggest problem is when a user needs employee overrides
